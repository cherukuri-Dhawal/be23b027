Strategies for Professional Growth

BSGN3001 | IIT Madras BS Degree in Data Science and Applications

Team:
Cherukuri Dhawal (BE23B027) | Tanushree Jagtap (BS23B026) | Muralitharan (BE24B010)

Introduction

  • Surveyed student career planning, time management, and stress.
  • Combined primary data (17-question survey) with expert interviews.
  • Developed practical roadmap for student growth.

Methods

  • Secondary research: academic articles, TED Talks, news clips.
  • Primary survey conducted across diverse student cohorts.
  • Key interviews: Dr. Sampath Kumar (VIT) & Mr. Prakash Dev (Electra).
  • Data modeling & visualization: statistical models, influence diagrams.

Findings & Results

  • Avg. study time ~3 hrs/day; top stressors—academic (70 %), career uncertainty (67 %), time pressure (42 %).
  • Coping mechanisms (music, social support, journaling) improved mental balance by ~50 %.
  • Task categorization & prioritization reduced deadline stress by ~40 %.

Conclusions

  • Career workshops & mentorship increased clarity & reduced future-related stress.
  • Digital planning tools effectively lowered time-pressure stress.
  • Peer support & self-care programs enhanced coping ability by 30–50 %.
  • Roadmap provides actionable steps for sustained student growth.

References

  • Seery, K.L. et al. (2010). “Whatever Does Not Kill Us…” J. Personality & Social Psych.
  • Hedau, S. (2025). Career Confusion among Indian Students. LinkedIn.
  • “Counselling a crucial step missed…” Times of India (2025).
